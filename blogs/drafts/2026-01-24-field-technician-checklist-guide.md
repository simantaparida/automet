## What Is a Field Technician Checklist? (Simple Definition)

**A field technician checklist is a structured, sequential list of tasks that technicians follow to ensure every job is completed safely, consistently, thoroughly, and with proper documentation ‚Äî eliminating guesswork and preventing errors.**

Think of it as a **step-by-step recipe** for technical work.

### **What Checklists Cover**

**Pre-Job Tasks**
- Review work order details
- Verify tools and parts needed
- Check safety equipment (PPE, lockout devices)
- Confirm site access and customer contact

**On-Site Tasks**
- Equipment identification and verification
- Safety procedures (lockout/tagout, power isolation)
- Diagnostic steps
- Repair or maintenance tasks in correct sequence
- Testing and verification

**Documentation Tasks**
- Capture before/after photos
- Record meter readings
- Log parts used
- Collect customer signature
- Update asset service history

**Post-Job Tasks**
- Verify problem resolution
- Clean work area
- Schedule follow-up if needed
- Complete digital report

---

### **Manual Checklists (Traditional)**

- Printed on paper or laminated cards
- Technician checks boxes with pen
- Stored in filing cabinets after completion
- Difficult to search or analyze
- Photos taken separately (often lost)

### **Digital Checklists (Modern)**

- Accessed on smartphones or tablets
- Checkboxes ticked in mobile app
- Photos captured and linked automatically
- GPS and timestamp recorded
- Data syncs to central system instantly
- Reports generated automatically

**The difference:** Manual checklists provide structure. Digital checklists provide structure **plus** accountability, proof, and analytics.

## What a Good Technician Checklist Should Include

Comprehensive checklists contain these essential elements:

### **1. Job Details**

- Work order number
- Customer name and location
- Asset identification (model, serial number)
- Job type (repair, preventive maintenance, installation)
- Priority level

**Why**: Ensures technician working on correct equipment.

---

### **2. Tools & Parts Required**

- List of necessary tools
- Required spare parts
- Consumables needed (lubricants, filters, etc.)
- Special equipment (meters, diagnostic tools)

**Why**: Prevents arriving unprepared. Reduces return trips for forgotten items.

---

### **3. Safety Instructions**

- Required PPE (hard hat, gloves, safety glasses)
- Lockout/tagout procedures
- Electrical isolation steps
- Chemical handling precautions
- Emergency contact information

**Why**: Protects technicians. Ensures regulatory compliance.

---

### **4. Step-by-Step Tasks**

- Diagnostic procedures (in logical sequence)
- Repair or maintenance steps (numbered)
- Settings to check or adjust
- Components to replace
- Cleaning procedures

**Why**: Guides systematic work. Prevents skipped steps.

---

### **5. Testing & Verification Steps**

- Performance tests to run
- Readings to verify (pressure, voltage, temperature)
- Functional tests (run cycles, operational checks)
- Acceptance criteria (what values are normal)

**Why**: Confirms work completed successfully before leaving site.

---

### **6. Proof-of-Work Requirements**

- Photos required (before, during, after)
- Meter readings to record
- Customer signature needed
- GPS timestamp requirement

**Why**: Documents work performed. Prevents disputes. Audit-ready.

---

### **7. Asset Information Fields**

- Operating hours or cycles
- Condition assessment
- Issues found (not just addressed)
- Recommendations for future maintenance

**Why**: Builds complete asset history. Enables predictive maintenance.

---

### **8. Technician Signature or Confirmation**

- Digital signature
- Certification that all steps completed
- Acknowledgment of safety procedures followed

**Why**: Accountability. Quality assurance.

---

### **9. Next Maintenance Due Date**

- When next service required
- What type of maintenance (PM schedule)
- Special notes for next visit

**Why**: Ensures continuity. Prevents forgotten maintenance.

## Mistakes Technicians Make Without Checklists

**Result**: Incomplete work. Customer callbacks. Wasted trips.

**Checklist Solution**: Proof requirements built into workflow. Can't close job without photos.

### **‚ùå Mistake 3: Incorrect Troubleshooting**

**Result**: Symptom treated, not problem. Equipment fails again soon.

**Checklist Solution**: Systematic diagnostic steps. Test all potential issues in order.

### **‚ùå Mistake 4: Performing Work in Wrong Order**

**Result**: Rework needed. Damage to equipment. Safety risks.

**Checklist Solution**: Numbered steps in logical sequence. Prevents mistakes.

### **‚ùå Mistake 5: Using Incorrect Tools**

**Problem**: Technician improvises with wrong tools.

**Result**: Stripped fasteners. Damaged components. Injury risk.

**Checklist Solution**: Required tools listed upfront. Technician arrives prepared.

---

### **‚ùå Mistake 6: Missing Safety Steps**

**Problem**: Safety procedures skipped to save time.

**Result**: Injuries. Regulatory violations. Liability.

**Checklist Solution**: Safety steps mandatory. System flags if skipped.

---

### **‚ùå Mistake 7: Poor Documentation**

**Problem**: Vague notes. Missing details. No useful history.

**Result**: Next technician starts from scratch. Repeated mistakes.

**Checklist Solution**: Structured data entry. Specific fields required.

---

### **‚ùå Mistake 8: No Asset History Awareness**

**Problem**: Technician doesn't know what was done before.

**Result**: Repeats unsuccessful repairs. Misses patterns.
